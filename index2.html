Who We Are

  • Story
  • Team
  • Ingredients

During my career in the food industry, working on farms to street food markets, I fell in love with the flavour and spices of Cajun chicken. So I started teaming up with chefs to create authentic recipes, constantly looking to New Orleans for inspiration and recreating their wonderful food over here. The flavours we created and feeling it provides is so good that we know everyone has to taste it.

Freshly made cajun seasoning with cayenne pepper, paprika and herbs; slowly marinated British chicken breasts, grilled, and finished with melting American cheese; Mama’s habanero chilli jam made with bell peppers, garlic and ginger; creamy slaw with a zingy dressing; double-cooked fries dusted in home made cajun seasoning - these are some of our recipes lovingly prepared and served with crusty on the outside, and pillowy soft on the inside french-style bread made down the road in South West London.

I launched Voodoo Chicken to prove that you can serve food that tastes amazing, that takes you on a journey, and that brings people together. I want to make it easy for people to eat well, at great value, and create memories that they’ll cherish forever. As they say in New Orleans, "Laissez les bons temps rouler" or "let the good times roll"!

Rory started Voodoo Chicken to take people on a taste journey, and to remind people what’s good about life.

Rory wants to bring people together because he believes it helps us to realise that we’re all the same, and that lesson teaches us that the only purpose of life is to enjoy it. There's no better way to bring people together than through food, which is far more than just fuel. It's a source of pleasure and something to share.

The flavours of New Orleans and stories from it's rich cultural history are absent from the UK's street food scene. Rory's goal is for people to discover the incredible flavours and aromatics of New Orleans street food, whilst taking them on a journey to the warm evenings, party atmosphere, and easy-going lifestyle of America's most historic city.

Working towards this vision is a growing group of amazing people. They are part of our family forever and have a spot on the Voodoo Chicken Wall of Fame.

We’ve worked hard to match the flavours of rural Louisiana with ingredients we can get hold of to create an authentic menu with ingredients sourced as locally as possible, or grown and transported with care for the environment.

We try to balance healthy eating with hearty eating, and wherever we can we source our supplies from small family run businesses that make the best product we can buy. We're passionate about our food and work closely with our suppliers to continually improve the quality and taste of the food we offer.

Our vegetables are sourced daily from London's New Covent Garden Market and our chicken comes from farms that we have visited in Lincolnshire. All our bread comes from an artisan bakery down the road in South West London.

Our stall is entirely hand-painted, and our bunting and aprons are hand-printed by local printers.
